Research program

My goal is to develop a reusable mathematical and algorithmic framework for fast and reliable generative inference, especially in settings where geometry, structure, or symmetry matters. I aim to connect theory, algorithms, and reproducible empirical evaluation (compute–quality–stability tradeoffs).

Pillar A — Coupling/bridge design for few-step generative inference

  • Reframe few-step generation around coupling/bridge choices and their failure modes.
  • Design and evaluate coupling families that improve quality under tight step budgets.

Pillar B — Quality-aligned certificates for inexact solvers

  • Define runtime certificates that track (and predict) quality/stability degradation under approximation.
  • Use certificates for adaptive step control (early stopping, solver switching, budget allocation).

Pillar C — Discrete + symmetry: structured-space generative inference

  • Extend few-step + stability ideas to discrete domains (tokens/graphs/sets) with symmetry constraints.
  • Connect structured generative operators to invariance/equivariance requirements and measurable stability criteria.
